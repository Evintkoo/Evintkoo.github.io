Keynesian ABM, zero lower bound, Minsky dynamics, fiscal policy, Rust, Monte Carlo
Daily-frequency, 10,000-agent closed-economy ABM reproducing four stylised macroeconomic facts. Novel result: ZLB binds in 93.7% of simulation periods (MC median), mirroring post-1998 Japan and post-2013 Euro-area — with bimodal distribution driven by TFP-variance threshold effects.

Individual Optimality and Collective Failure in a Keynesian Agent-Based Model
Computational Economics · Coordination Failure
coordination failure, grid search, survivability optimisation, decentralised equilibrium, Keynesian ABM
Grid search identifies survival-maximising strategies for households, firms, and banks. The all-best economy — all types simultaneously following individually-optimal rules — produces GDP growth −65%, firm bankruptcies 17× higher, and negative mean firm profit: a structural coordination failure attractor.
